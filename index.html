Team Transportation Transformers · Las Positas College · Livermore, CA

Turn the campus into a solar power plant that funds clean transportation.

Transportation is the single largest source of Las Positas' carbon emissions. The plan: roughly double our solar to about 4.35 MW by adding carport canopies over the parking lots, free up the ~$1M/year we pay PG&E, and reinvest that money into free EV charging, better transit, and dividends that reward going electric.

Risk Register

Challenges and open questions

A candid accounting of what could complicate or delay the project.

Grid

PG&E grid upgrade costs

Interconnection-driven upgrades are unknown until the Rule 21 study completes, potentially a six-figure swing in net cost.

Market

NEM 3.0 low export rates

Export compensation near 9 cents is far below the 43 cent retail rate, so on-site use and storage matter more than selling back.

Operations

CAISO scheduling complexity

Operating as a registered DG facility adds market scheduling and metering obligations beyond a simple net-metered system.

Labor

Union negotiations

New faculty and staff parking fees ($99 gas, $50 EV) require bargaining, a political and contractual hurdle, not just a billing change.

Safety

Battery fire code

Adding storage triggers fire-code setbacks, ventilation, and inspection requirements that raise cost and siting complexity.

Policy

Federal funding instability

ITC levels and federal incentives can shift with policy, so the $1.43M grant assumption carries real exposure.

Governance

Board turnover risk

A two-year timeline spans election cycles, and changing board priorities could stall or defund the project mid-stream.
